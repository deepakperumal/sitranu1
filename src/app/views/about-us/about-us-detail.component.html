History


It all started on a fine morning at the university dorm. While the rest of the world was celebrating and welcoming the new year 2017 cheerfully, we wished to do something remarkable in the new year, we wished to give back to the society, we were brainstorming for ideas. Eventually, we talked about how grateful we are for having the privilege to receive a quality education and how our life would have ended up without having that.

Then, we realized that there exist a plenty of young bright minds who have the potential to radically improve their life, provided they get a quality education. Unfortunately, however, many such young minds are born into the poor families in villages who cannot afford a quality education. We asked ourselves a question: why shouldn't we find such students and sponsor them entirely?

That question got stuck in our minds. And, our dear reader, as an answer to that question, Sitranu Foundation was born. An open, transparent, community-based foundation where members can donate money, take up volunteering work, bring in potential students, campaign and win the community vote for sponsorship. Basically, a foundation for the community and by the community. Like an Indian traditional baby, the foundation was not born with a name. We called it "Nameless Foundation" initially. In fact, that's how we promoted while adding our initial members. Later, we conducted a name-finding discussion and concluded on "Sitranu Foundation". Sitranu is a beautiful Tamil word, refers to a small atom. An atom that is so small and invisible by itself, but when bonded with several such atoms, it makes a great human being like you and the entire universe that you live in.

Similarly, the helping hands from the members of our foundation, even if it's a fraction of the whole, but when combined with others, makes a great significance. Every member in our foundation is, therefore, a Sitranu who helps in providing quality education for the needy students. Such a meaningful name for what our foundation is all about.


Our Mission


In the state of Tamil Nadu in India, every year around a million students appear for the Higher Secondary state examination. There exists a fierce competition among students to land in top universities. There are private schools that are reputed for preparing students to excel in their examinations, but are expensive. On the other hand, a large number of government schools in remote villages have limited infrastructure and a lethargic mindset in pushing students to excel, but it offers free education.

Families living in poverty cannot afford private schooling for their children. Therefore, very talented but poor students from villages lack good teaching and support to attend reputed universities. Our responsibility is to find such potential students and sponsor them for private school education.

Our mission is to provide an opportunity and uplift at least 100 students every year by 2025 and thereafter.


Our Strengths


  • A foundation owned by everyone. With members adding their friends and family after gaining our trust, our foundation is growing steadily and in a sustainable manner. Members also volunteer.

  • We do not do paid advertising, neither have paid employees. 99% of the donations reach the students directly, the remaining 1% is used for the domain name of this website, or other similar expenses.

  • We only act as a platform bringing together the students and the donors. You get a very personal touch, if you’re interested, by visiting or talking to the students and their parents periodically, mentoring them, getting regular updates on the student’s academic progress and so on.

  • We intend to find the talents in their early age around 6th or 7th standard and prepare them for the final 12th standard giving ample time.

  • We are aiming to be very transparent with income and expenses, and democratic in our decisions. Everyone gets an annual report with greater details on the finances. All the decisions are taken based on member voting. So, you have a say in who gets the sponsorship, for example.

  • We’re already global, with donors from India, Germany, China, Singapore, and the USA.
